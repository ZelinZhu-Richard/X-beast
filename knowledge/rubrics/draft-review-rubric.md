# Draft Review Rubric

Use this rubric to score a full draft after hook selection and before final polish.

## Scoring

Score each dimension from 1 to 10. Total possible score: 80.

| Dimension | What 1 Looks Like | What 10 Looks Like |
|-----------|-------------------|--------------------|
| Hook Strength | bland, confusing, or overpromising | immediately earns attention and sets the right expectation |
| Clarity | the point gets buried or blurred | the main claim is unmistakable |
| Specificity | vague claims and generic language | concrete wording, clear distinctions, and grounded examples |
| Technical Truth | sloppy, overstated, or easy to falsify | accurate, bounded, and defensible |
| Business Value | no practical payoff for builders or operators | gives the reader a sharper evaluation or decision lens |
| Voice Fit | generic, hypey, or too polished | sharp, human, direct, and credible |
| Compactness | too long for the value delivered | as short as possible without losing truth or usefulness |
| Reply Potential | dead-end statement with no room for smart discussion | invites strong, relevant follow-up from the right readers |

## Decision Bands

- `68+` and no blocker comments: Publish-ready after polish.
- `56-67`: Revise.
- `<56`: Redraft.

## Blocker Rule

Blocker comments outrank score totals.

- A high score does not cancel a blocker from `karpathy-reviewer`.
- A sharp hook does not save a strategically empty post.
- A polished voice score does not justify cutting truth-preserving detail.

## Notes

- Score the draft that exists, not the better version you imagine after rewriting it.
- Compactness should never be improved by removing a qualifier required for truth or usefulness.
