# Hook Rubric

Use this rubric to choose between multiple opening lines before drafting the full post.

## Scoring

Score each dimension from 1 to 10. Total possible score: 60.

| Dimension | What 1 Looks Like | What 10 Looks Like |
|-----------|-------------------|--------------------|
| Clarity | reader cannot tell what the hook is claiming | the point is immediately legible |
| Specificity | generic statement that could fit any post | concrete wording, sharp distinction, or clear context |
| Tension | flat or predictable | creates real stakes, contrast, or curiosity |
| Credibility | empty authority or unsupported boldness | sounds believable because the framing feels earned |
| Audience Fit | wrong reader or too broad to matter | speaks directly to the intended reader |
| Continuation Pull | no reason to read line two | naturally makes the reader want the rest of the post |

## Reject Hooks That

- use vague curiosity bait with no real idea behind it
- imply authority that the brief cannot support
- only make sense after the body explains what the hook meant
- sound like a template instead of a real opinion or observation

## Notes

- A strong hook should be sharp without becoming cryptic.
- Hooks that score high on tension but low on credibility usually collapse during review.
