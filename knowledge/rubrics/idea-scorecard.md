# Idea Scorecard

Use this scorecard to decide whether a brief is worth drafting today.

## Scoring

Score each dimension from 1 to 10. Total possible score: 60.

| Dimension | What 1 Looks Like | What 10 Looks Like |
|-----------|-------------------|--------------------|
| Novelty | obvious, overfarmed, or interchangeable with dozens of other posts | fresh angle or sharper framing than the market is currently using |
| Proof / Source Strength | no firsthand basis, weak sourcing, or pure opinion | grounded in firsthand observation, credible notes, or strong source material |
| Audience Fit | vague audience or wrong audience for the account | clearly built for the exact reader the account wants to attract |
| Business Relevance | interesting but strategically empty | clearly tied to product, buying, operating, or market judgment |
| Clarity Potential | the idea is hard to explain cleanly in one post | the idea can be stated cleanly and defended in X-native form |
| Discussion Potential | informative but unlikely to trigger smart replies | likely to generate useful disagreement, examples, or operator discussion |

## Decision Bands

- `50-60`: Draft today.
- `40-49`: Strong backlog.
- `30-39`: Revise the angle before drafting.
- `<30`: Do not draft.

## Notes

- An idea can be strong without being universally novel if the proof is strong and the audience fit is exact.
- "Discussion potential" means the post is likely to draw thoughtful replies, not empty agreement bait.
- Do not rescue a weak-proof idea just because the hook could be catchy.
