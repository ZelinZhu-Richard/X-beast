# Postmortem Rubric

Use this rubric after a post has been published and enough response data exists to learn from it.

## Scoring

Score each dimension from 1 to 10. Total possible score: 60.

| Dimension | What 1 Looks Like | What 10 Looks Like |
|-----------|-------------------|--------------------|
| Outcome Diagnosis | vague summary with no clear read on what happened | clear explanation of what worked, what failed, and why |
| Evidence Quality | guesses based on vibes | grounded in metrics, replies, saves, and observable behavior |
| Pattern Recognition | treats each post as a one-off | identifies repeatable patterns across multiple posts |
| Audience Insight | no real read on reader behavior | clear sense of what resonated with the intended audience |
| Next-Step Usefulness | no operational consequence | leads directly to better hooks, drafts, or review choices |
| Experiment Quality | random next action | one clean test that can validate or falsify the lesson |

## Decision Bands

- `50-60`: Strong learning. Update the room's working standards.
- `40-49`: Useful learning. Carry one or two changes into the next cycle.
- `30-39`: Weak learning. The observations are too vague.
- `<30`: Do not treat this as a reliable postmortem.

## Required Outputs

- `Repeat:` one thing to keep doing
- `Stop:` one thing to stop doing
- `Test:` one thing to test next
