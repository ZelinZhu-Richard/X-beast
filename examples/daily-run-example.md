## Hook Options

1. "The most misleading part of an AI demo is that the hard part has not happened yet."
2. "After testing AI tools on the same spec-to-revision workflow, I stopped caring how good the first demo looked."
3. "Flashy AI demos are getting overvalued. For teams, the real moat is whether the tool survives revisions without falling apart."

## Hook Choice

Hook 2 wins.

It signals firsthand experience, names the evaluation lens, and gives the body a clear path: first-pass output is easy to impress with, revision-heavy work is where the real product quality shows up.

## Draft Option A

Flashy AI demos are overrated.

The first pass is the easiest part of the product to fake.

The real test is what happens on revision 3, when requirements change and edge cases show up.

If the tool falls apart there, the demo was a stage trick.

Teams should evaluate AI products on iteration quality, not first-pass wow.

## Draft Option B

I have mostly stopped judging AI tools by the first demo.

On the first pass, a lot of tools look impressive.

The real gap shows up when you change the requirements, tighten the constraints, and push through a few ugly revisions.

If a tool breaks there, the demo did not tell you enough.

Teams should evaluate AI tools on revision reliability, not first-pass flash.

## Chosen Draft Sent To Review

Draft B is the better review candidate.

- It is more defensible.
- It still preserves a strong opinion.
- It gives reviewers something concrete to sharpen instead of something inflated to tone down.
